Mental Health Literacy

What is it? Why is it important? Mental health literacy is simply the adequate understanding of mental illness and it's prevalence throughout the world. Mental health literacy encourages non-judgmental awareness and compassion through the destigmatization of mental health disorders nationwide and beyond. As we progress into the new millenium, awareness is becoming more important rather than less important due to the widespread prevalence of the disease and the many ways in which it manifests through the human psyche.

Here is a list of symptoms to look out for and speak to your healthcare provider about if you are exeriencing three or more for longer than two weeks:
  1. Trouble sleeping
  2. Loss of interest
  3. Increase in energy
  4. Change in appetite
  5. Irritabilty
  6. Hopelessness
Now take a look at the following preventative measures one can take in order to keep his/her mental health in check:
  • Healthy diet
  • Increased physical activity
  • Improving social network
  • Participating in structured hobbies
  • Volunteering
